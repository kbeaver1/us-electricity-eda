# us-electricity-eda
This report was created as background research in applying for an business internship with the Tucson Electric Power (TEP) company in 2024. To understand more about the commodity price environment that TEP operates in and what affected their sales the most, I gathered wholesale daily spot prices from the U.S. Energy Information Administration from 2014-2024. 

I discovered that spot prices are highly correlated with changes in temperature and energy consumption, which is how the electric industry operates, but in one rare case I discovered that an $84 million dollar Power Purchase Agreement in June 2021 affected short-term sales for the Palo Verde Peak (PVP), one of the largest electric hubs within TEP's electric system. The figure represents the change in PVP spot prices from June 1-30, 2021.
